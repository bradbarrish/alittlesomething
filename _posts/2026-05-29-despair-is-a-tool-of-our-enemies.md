As I write this at the end of May in the year 2026, things are a bit bleak. But I came across a great post by Dan Sinker called *[A pep talk in the face of despair (or: trying redux)](https://dansinker.com/posts/2026-05-14-trying-redux/)*. You should read the whole thing, but here’s the bit that got me:

>It's not even optimistic to say we have to try, _it's just necessary_. To give up, to stop trying, is to accept that things can't change. As Audre Lorde put it: ["despair is a tool of our enemies."](https://queerhistory.com/radical-graduation) Despair is a gift to those that want you to believe that what we've got is all we'll get and there's no way to make things better.
