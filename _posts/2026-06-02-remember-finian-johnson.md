June 2, 2026 was a Tuesday. And it was a beautiful day, but it was also a gut-wrenchingly sad day for many people. It was the day of Finian Johnson’s funeral at the Hollywood Forever Cemetery in Los Angeles. We were all there as a family to show our support for Finian’s family and friends. It didn’t matter that we didn’t know him well. It didn’t matter that you weren’t friends with his kids. What mattered was that we showed up. Maybe no one in the family will remember that we were there. Who could really blame them. But you were there and even if you don’t remember much about his funeral or any of the touching words people had to say or his friend that covered *Brokedown Palace* by The Grateful Dead. That one really got me. 

I think one of the most difficult things to learn in life is just how precious it can be. Unfortunately, it often takes a tragedy to remind us just how lucky we are. We never really know when our time will come. I say that, not to scare you or to make you worry about it, but to encourage you to live your best life. Dream your dreams! Tell your friends you love them every time you talk to them! Go have the dinner! Go to the concert! Stay up a little later with your friends! Surround yourself with friends and family. It is truly what makes life. It has very little to do with the things you have. In fact, the more things you have often makes people less happy. 

By all accounts, Fin lived life. He loved hard. From listening to what people said about him, I wish I would have gotten to know him better. I certainly admired his style, always felt welcomed by him and he so clearly loved his family. Him and his wife, Mayara seemed to be madly in love and their kids, Van and Mac, were everything to him.
